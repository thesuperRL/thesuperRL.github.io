Genius Hour

Creating things in the EPS Makerspace

One of my favorite things to do is to make things in the EPS makerspace. I began in 9th grade working on small projects with 3D printing, and started by learning the two types of 3D printers here. Later, I transitioned to other materials, mostly after I entered the 10th grade.

Now, I know how to use many of the makerspace machines. These are the machines that I often use. (Click to expand)

Lately, I have been increasing my skills with 3D modeling in order to create more impressive projects. I now mainly use Fusion 360, and I am working on modeling the organization of a car trunk for the EPS EV project. With this, we can adequately plan out the locations of the future electronics. I am also receiving the first requests for me to make something, starting from a "commission" of a wicket a few days ago.

My goals with this skill is to create:

  • A "Layered Laser Cut" with pieces stacked on each other to create depth
  • A functional 3D print that involves gears to have a purpose
  • Proficiency with the Resin Printer
  • Start a shop to sell some of the products I make
